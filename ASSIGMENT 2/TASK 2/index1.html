Tribute to Nikola Tesla

About Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856â€“1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who made groundbreaking contributions to the development of the modern electrical system. Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia), Tesla emigrated to the United States in 1884 where he worked with Thomas Edison before pursuing his own revolutionary ideas. He is best known for his development of alternating current (AC) electrical system, which became the standard for power transmission worldwide. Tesla held over 300 patents and his inventions continue to influence technology to this day.

Key Events in Tesla's Life

  1. 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire
  2. 1884 - Emigrated to the United States
  3. 1888 - Patented the AC induction motor and transformer
  4. 1891 - Invented the Tesla Coil
  5. 1893 - Demonstrated wireless radio communication
  6. 1943 - Died in New York City at age 86

Major Inventions & Contributions

  • Alternating Current (AC) electrical system
  • Tesla Coil (high-voltage transformer)
  • Induction motor
  • Wireless communication technology
  • Radio remote control
  • Contributions to X-ray technology
